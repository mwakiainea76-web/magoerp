Recognized straightforward financial definition query
Recognized straightforward financial definition query
An invoice is a formal document a seller sends to a buyer, requesting payment for goods or services that have been provided. It's essentially a bill — but with specific details that make it a proper financial and legal record.

What an invoice typically includes:

Invoice number (unique identifier for tracking)
Date issued and payment due date
Seller's and buyer's details (names, addresses, contact info)
Description of goods/services provided
Quantity and unit price of each item
Total amount owed (often broken into subtotal, taxes, discounts, and grand total)
Payment terms (e.g. "Net 30" means payment is due 30 days after the invoice date)
Accepted payment methods
Why it matters:

Legal record — proof that a transaction took place and what was agreed.
Accounting — used to record revenue (for the seller) and expenses/liabilities (for the buyer).
Cash flow tracking — helps businesses know what's owed to them (accounts receivable) and what they owe (accounts payable).
Tax purposes — invoices support tax filings and audits.
Invoice vs. related terms (often confused):

Term	Meaning
Quote/Estimate	Sent before work is done, not a request for payment yet
Invoice	Sent after goods/services are delivered, requesting payment
Receipt	Proof that payment has already been made
Purchase Order (PO)	Sent by the buyer to the seller, ordering goods/services
In the context of your school fee system — an invoice would typically be the document generated per student, per session, listing the fees owed for that session, when they're due, and how much (if any) has already been paid.

Prompt: Implement Dual-Mode Fee Issuance (Per-Session & Per-Year) with Session Lifecycle and Flexible Fee Statements

Context
The system is session-based, with 3 sessions per academic year by default (configurable — see prior refactor). We now need to support two fee issuance modes that coexist on top of the session-based structure, plus flexible statement views for students/sponsors.

1. Academic Year & Session Setup

When an admin creates a new academic year, the system must automatically generate its 3 sessions (or however many are configured via SESSIONS_PER_ACADEMIC_YEAR).
All 3 sessions are created with status inactive by default.
Only one session at a time can be marked active — this is the session students are currently allowed to register under.
Activating a session should (by business rule) deactivate any other currently active session in that year — clarify/enforce single-active-session-per-year (or system-wide, if applicable).
2. Fee Issuance Modes
Finance can issue course fees in two modes:

Per-Session Fee: A fee issued for a single, specific session only. Applies and is payable within that session.
Per-Year Fee: A fee issued once, at the time the academic year starts, intended to cover all 3 sessions in that year. When issued:
The system splits the total fee across the 3 sessions using a configurable ratio (default: equal split — 1/3 per session; but ratio should be adjustable per fee/course, e.g. 40/30/30, in config or at issuance time).
A fee record is created and stored for each of the 3 sessions, including sessions that are still inactive.
Fee records tied to inactive sessions remain dormant/pending — not payable or visible in active billing — until their session is activated.
When a session transitions from inactive → active, its associated pre-stored fee portion becomes live/active (visible for payment, included in current balance, etc.).
3. Data Model Implications

A fee record must track:
issuance_type: per_session or per_year
parent_fee_id (or similar): links session-level fee portions back to the original per-year fee issuance, so they can be grouped/reported together.
session_id: which session this specific portion applies to.
amount: the amount allocated to that session (post-split if per-year).
status: reflects whether this portion is dormant (session inactive) or active/live (session active).
Ensure the split amounts sum exactly to the original total fee (watch for rounding errors — e.g. remainder should be added to the last session's portion).
4. Fee Statements
Students (and their sponsors) must be able to view a fee statement in three modes:

Default — Session-to-Date: Shows fee/payment activity from session 1 up to the current date (i.e., cumulative across sessions to date, not just the currently active session).
Per Session: Shows fee/payment breakdown for a single, selected session only.
Per Year: Shows the full academic year's fee breakdown — aggregating all 3 sessions' worth of fees (whether originally issued per-session or as a split per-year fee) into one statement. This is critical for sponsors who require the full year's balance rather than session-by-session detail.
Each statement view should clearly show, at minimum:

Total fees charged (for the selected scope)
Total paid
Outstanding balance
Breakdown by session (for the per-year and session-to-date views)
Indication of which portions are still dormant/pending (session not yet active), if relevant to the sponsor/student view
5. Acceptance Criteria

Creating an academic year auto-generates 3 inactive sessions.
Only one session can be active at a time.
Finance can issue a fee as either per-session or per-year at course-fee creation.
Per-year fees are split and stored against all 3 sessions immediately, with inactive-session portions dormant until activation.
Activating a session automatically activates its associated dormant fee portions.
Fee statements support all three view modes (session-to-date default, per session, per year) with accurate aggregation.
Split ratios are configurable, not hardcoded to equal thirds.
No double-counting or missing amounts when aggregating per-year statements from session-level records.
6. Open Questions to Clarify Before Building

Is "active session" scoped per academic year, or is there only ever one active session system-wide across all years?
Can a per-year fee be issued mid-year (after session 1 has already started), or only at the very start of the academic year? If mid-year, how should the split/backdating work?
Should dormant fee portions be visible to students/sponsors at all (e.g., "upcoming" fees), or completely hidden until activated?
What happens to a per-year fee split if the ratio or total is edited after issuance but before all sessions are activated?
